Group processes

In general, our group did fine when working together in Assignment 2. Here are some good tactics in Assignment 2, that are used again in the Assignment 3:

Subjective:
  • Schedule bonding time to let team members understanding each others and helps them get out of their “comfort zone”, so the team can maximize the outcome of the project

  • Letting members choose their tasks before assigning them.

  • Getting to know other members’ schedules and their life outside of school work to sympathize and support if bad things happen.

Objective:
  • The tasks are split and assigned equally and fairly among team members.

  • Each task will have its own deadline as we also consider the workload of each task differently.

  • The deadlines are set a little sooner than when they should be to create courage for team members to finish the task and also give them the feelings they have extra time in case the deadlines are not met.

  • Each task will be assigned based on team members’ skills, experiences, and knowledges about that tasks

  • One can join and support other team members on a task if they want to learn more about that particular task.

  • When finished, tasks are reviewed by other members to not only improve the quality of the finished tasks, but also its synergy with others’ works.

On the other hand, we also have some bad practices that need to be fixed. Here are those bad practices and our solutions:

Subjective:
  • Miscommunications happened due to busy schedules, which resulted in stress and delays - Spending more time checking on each other to minimize miscommunications.

  • Procrastination happened due to many reasons - Schedule working time offline so all members can gather together and encourage each other to work

Overview

Overview: Our project consists of 2 sections which are the technical part and non-technical part and in each section, there will be more parts of it. For the technical part, there are 6 sections which we propose to do. The first section is the login system, the second is the quiz/survey/questionnaires, the third part is online chat services, the fourth part is online interpreter, the fifth part is online judging tool and the final part is the forum. For the non-technical part, there are 4 sections that we intend to do. Outcome: If CodeX is successful, there might be more and more people working in the IT industry which meet the requirement of this career for the next 10 years. Therefore, the IT industry in Vietnam will be developed and reach a position that could be compared to other developed countries such as China, America, Russia, etc. Consequently, robots for housework which helps saving time, self-driving cars which are very useful for those who do not know to drive cars, virtual assistants like Jarvis in Marvel movies to arrange your work,.. those things in the next 5 years might be common in Vietnam. Finally, with those services made in Vietnam, it can be bought from other countries which can bring benefits to Vietnam’s economic efficiency.


2. Motivation:

We are now standing on the brick of four industrial revolutions. Therefore, the demand for computer and information technology has been currently increasing. According to VietNam’s News, Viet Nam needs more than 400,000 information technology (IT) in 2020 and this number will become greater for the next 10 years. Not all Vietnamese people have the chance to study English so learning through video tutorials or reading documents is quite hard for them to understand especially for the young. This application will be an essential tool for the start of exploring the IT world. Moreover, for those who want to change their career path to IT or lost basic knowledge about IT, CodeX provides many courses which take 3-4 hours to help them keep back on track.

Detail Desciption


Aims

According to Viet Nam News, the Information Technology (IT) industry’s demand for IT workers in Vietnam will reach an outstanding number of 400,000 by the year of 2020. If this prediction is valid, there will be a surge of people who want to study or switch their career paths to IT. With that in mind, our aim is to create a web-based platform that provides excellent education and after-study activities about coding and programming (we believe this is the gateway to IT) with a focus on Vietnamese users. In order to achieve our aim, we have to set a list of specific goals that we have to fulfil.

Firstly, due to inadequate skills or lack of foreign language education (English), it is important for us to support our Vietnamese users with both English and Vietnamese for deeper understanding of the content they are studying. This goal requires us to have not only a good understanding of our content but also good English skills, as we have to deliver the content in both English and Vietnamese without altering the meaning of our contents. For this goal, we may need the help and advice from English language experts to accomplish it.

Secondly, we would love to have an online programming language compiler on our website. The ability to not only learn the content but also try and execute it right away is important to us. Our team is aware of the existence of many free, both online and offline compilers out there. However, we still want to have our own online compiler integrated in our website for the convenience of our users.

Another goal of our team is to provide good after-study activities for our users like quizzes, mentorings, projects, competitions, connections, and so on. These after-study activities aim to strengthen the understanding of users on the content the project provides. These activities also give them more hands-on experience and prepare them for the real-world IT industry. For example, by forming teams with other users and participating in competitions, users have the chance to apply the knowledge they learn to solve real-world problems. As attending competitions is one of many good ways that helps users prove their abilities, it secures the chance in landing their dream jobs and strengthening their career paths.

As our project’s main focus is the users in Vietnam, providing a Vietnamese content for this platform is a must-have. If things do not go as planned, we will go out of our way to make sure that this feature is fulfilled as our main focus is to help coding and programming reach as many Vietnamese people as possible.

Plans and Progress

Although we decided to have CodeX as our group project, the original story behind CodeX is from Quynh’s idea after years of coding/programming self-study. Quynh shared with us that the free and available content for learning programming languages online is really good and helpful; however, there is still room for improvements. As most of the well-known web applications in this category are in English, it is really hard for Vietnamese users, especially those who do not have proper English skills but really want to study coding and programming. This creates a big barrier for them, and in results, limiting the number of users in this category. Even for Quynh, who has adequate English skills in general, she still has trouble understanding the concepts and languages during her self-study time. As Vietnam is entering its 4.0 technology era, even more and more people will think about applying for a job in the IT industry and CodeX is her and our group’s answer to that demand.

What your project will do ?

Our project would be a web application called CodeX that could help users in Vietnam learn how to code and program. This application will provide many walkthrough tutorials to learn different programming languages such as C++, Java, Python, etc. Moreover, at the end of each session, to know if the users understand what they have learned, CodeX will provide several quizzes to help users remember the course easier. Besides that, it will have many mentors available for you to ask how to start coding, which is the best language for you to learn. Moreover, you can match with somebody that starts the same program path as you. Therefore, you can learn something new from others and tell them about what you have learned because one of the best ways to learn something is teaching it to somebody else. If you finish our tutorials, there are many challenges or pet projects to do as practice makes perfect.

First of all, the data that needs to be looked after when handing over the project is the Team Profile. Testing

The project of the TAQ Team is about creating a web-based application, named CodeX, to provide an online learning space about coding, coding language, and programming for mainly Vietnamese users. According to Software Testing Help, web application testing has 6 major checklists and each checklist focuses on a different area of the web application [1]. Here are the 6 checklists for web application testing:
  • Functionality Testing

  • Usability Testing

  • Interface Testing

  • Compatibility Testing

  • Performance Testing

  • Security Testing



For the testing of our project, we will base this guideline to test it thoroughly. For example, in Functionality Testing, one of the required tasks is to double-check and ensure that all the links and inter-connections between pages are working accordingly [1]. Another good example is user’s navigation when using the website. In Usability Testing, the layout of the web application dictates how users will interact and navigate through it [1].

When the actual testing takes place, these guidelines have an important role in letting us know when the project has succeeded; it can be either the whole project, or even a small requirement in any of the 6 checklists above. As founders and creators, we, as a group, can also develop a set of standards and requirements of our own for our CodeX project. For example, one of the focuses for CodeX is providing coding/programming education in Vietnamese for the Vietnamese. We might set our goal to have the web application written with the percentage of 75% covered in Vietnamese. However, even if we succeed in this task, it does not mean that the whole project will be a complete success. These guidelines, standards, or requirements still only serve parts of the whole picture as there are many other variables that can affect the actual outcome of the project, especially those our group cannot predict and foresee.

In our testing, depending on the state of the project, prototypes and demos might be used as the testing tools to gain better and deeper feedback. Prototypes and demos can be produced specifically for a certain task/requirement. For example, if we want to test the effectiveness and efficiency of our web application’s interface, we can have the Designer design the layout of the website and use Adobe XD or Photoshop to illustrate the idea and how it actually looks before deploying it.

Speaking of user testing, it is also important to have a clear plan on how we find our test users and how they are qualified. Since all the team members are currently studying at RMIT University Vietnam, we can ask students and staff at the university to help us with the user testing. Before the actual testing, we need to do some surveys on our test users to get their background and other related information. This helps us classify them into sub-groups, which may have different characteristics and considerations from the main population. For the main population, we plan to increase the size of our test users’ base as the project progresses (around 10 to 20 people for the first batch of our prototype). We choose to do this because we want to limit the number of input feedbacks from test users during the early stage of the project, where it is still vague and undecided in terms of overall experience. This helps us in focusing on what matters and what we have set out to do instead of thinking and worrying about what test users want based on their personal experience at the early stage. It is important to note that this does not mean the feedback from test users are not important for the development of our project.

Risks

During the process of building a website, there might be some risk. The first risk is that for developing some features for CodeX, we have to use some languages programming which are hard to learn or we have not faced before. For example, if we develop an online interpreter, we have to use Nodejs which nobody in my group has learned. The second risk is that there are not enough resources to build up the features for the website, For example, while researching how to develop the online tool judgement, there is no document that guides us to do that instead of the open-source code. Aside from the technical issues, we might face problems related to human factors. Since our project needs the team members to put a lot of effort to do and the work keeps increasing in their head. Some people may lose their spirit and procrastinate which reduces working efficiency. The result of this might be that our project would never be done.
Another risk that can be foreseen is coding/programming the web application CodeX as a group. Although we have Github and Atom to manage our codes and how it should be committed to keeping the integrity of the program, different coding and programming styles can lead to bugs that are so hard to trace out. That is why companies always have coding guidelines or rules to shape their employees’ coding styles under the same structure. Professor Tran Ngoc Quang also talked about this solution during our interview with him for Assignment 2. Our group also plans to have a coding guideline for our own project if this project hits its next phase.


Group process and communications

Group communication is very important when doing our group project. It could help the project to be more efficient. When doing this group project, communication is unavoidable in order to perfect the project.

Tuan, Quynh and Ky Anh therefore were RMIT students who study at the same courses. This resulted in that when there are any lessons, the whole group will meet directly at the classroom, this was very convenient because the group does not have to schedule for the meeting, in fact the whole group can meet each other after class. Moreover, due to the current situation worldwide, personal issues as well as participating in other courses, the group has to minimize the meeting time at school. Therefore, the group decided to start a meeting via Team software more frequently. Twice a week including the weekend, besides meeting at the classroom the TAQ’s team will schedule and decide which day they will start the meeting online and which time the team will be most flexible to meet online. Furthermore, the meeting also took place outside the school. Nevertheless, each meeting will take approximately 2 hours at the maximum and half an hour if there are not any crucial things to discuss. The scheduled meeting for the whole week will be divided consecutively.


From Monday to Wednesday, the TAQ’s team will meet at school face-to-face. From Thursday to Sunday, the meeting will be held online or at some working place such as a coffee shop. The team leader will announce to the group via text on Messenger when there is any meeting deadline is coming so every member will have time to prepare and notice.

If a member cannot attend the meeting, the person who is absent has to inform the leader the reason for not participating in the meeting. After the meeting, the absent member will be announced by the team leader or other member via text or phone call on which sections or tasks they have to work on.
